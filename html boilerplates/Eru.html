A sweet Eru recipe

Nothing delivers flavor and finger-licking goodness with little fuss and even less time than Eru soup.
When I decided to start a cooking and lifestyle blog, the first recipe that crossed my mind was eru. It was at the top of my list of recipes to make, for the blog, because I have been cooking for a very long time and if there is any dish that I knoe how to cook really well, it’s Eru. So it would only feel natural to start with it. I kept putting it off, in favor of other new recipes.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds meat, 1½ pounds dried fish
  • Dried chopped eru leaves 5-6 cups
  • 2 pounds of frozen spinach or water leaves
  • Palm oil 2-4 cups,Ground crayfish ½ cup
  • 2 Maggi,Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Cut the beef in bite size chunks, season with salt, Maggi, and onions and cook until tender. Do same for the cow skin.
  2. Use very little water while cooking the meat because eru does not need a lot of water . However, make sure you have at least 2 to 3 cups of stock from the beef. to use in cooking this dish.
  3. While the meat is cooking soak the dried Ukazi/Eru leaves in water in a bowl to tenderize the leaves for approximately 3-4 minutes. Rinse and drain
  4. Add the assorted meats, cow skin, dried fish to a large stock pot with at least 2 cups of stock. Bring it to a boil and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. In a blender or food processor, combine garlic cloves, ginger, green onions, habanero, parsley and 1/4 cup of water. Blend until it forms a creamy fresh spice blend
  6. Arrange, slice and rinse your water leaves or spinach and add to the pot of assorted meat and fish.
  7. Add crayfish and 2 tablespoon bouillon/ Maggi at this point stir, let it cook on medium heat (approximately 2 minutes).
  8. Add shredded Ukazi/Eru leaves, and red oil. Stir again thoroughly making sure all the contents in the pot blend together .
  9. Simmer for another 10 minutes, add stock /water if needed . The fragrant smell that erupts from the dish lets you know it is time to eat.
  10. Season to taste with salt and more Maggi as needed. ( I usually season my food with salt and Maggie as the dish progresses .)

Totally yummy. It’s an eruption of flavours in one’s mouth.
